But **Dataset Distillation is not pixel compression; it is "concept" compression.** Here is exactly why the global model *must* be trained first, and why trying to distill the images with an untrained model caused your MMD loss to go crazy.

### 1. The Model is the "Translator"

In Feature Distribution Matching (DM), the algorithm never actually compares the pixels of your fake image to the pixels of the real image.

Instead, it passes both images through the neural network and compares the **Feature Embeddings** (the deep mathematical vectors that the convolutional layers produce).

The neural network acts as a translator. It reads raw pixels and translates them into "concepts."

* **If the model is TRAINED:** It knows how to translate. It looks at a real image and says, *"Ah, I see a round collar and short sleeves. That's a T-shirt."* When you pass the fake noise image in, the optimizer can successfully tweak the noise until the model says, *"Okay, now the noise also looks like a round collar and short sleeves."*
* **If the model is UNTRAINED:** The weights are completely random. It is like a translator who doesn't speak the language. It looks at a real T-shirt and outputs random mathematical gibberish. It looks at the fake noise and outputs different random gibberish. Because the "target" (the real image's features) is just random garbage, the optimizer has nothing meaningful to aim at. The MMD loss gets stuck or explodes because it is trying to match random noise to random noise.

### 2. The LoRA Adapters Need the Same "Brain"

Remember what happens in Phase 2 (Unlearning). You are freezing the trained `global_model` and injecting LoRA adapters into its deep layers.

For the LoRA adapters to work, the synthetic images they are looking at must perfectly trigger the exact same neural pathways that the real images would trigger in *that specific trained model*. If you distill the images using an untrained model, the synthetic images will be completely incompatible with the trained model during the unlearning phase.
